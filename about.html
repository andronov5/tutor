Meet Lis

Lis Cohen is an experienced educator who helps students feel calmer, clearer, and more confident.

She has taught and trained students, scientists, engineers, policy makers, and public audiences. She also works part-time with the COMET program at the University Corporation for Atmospheric Research.

Lis has a master’s degree in meteorology from the University of Utah and a master’s degree in public affairs from Princeton University. She earned her bachelor’s degree at Cornell University, where she studied Atmospheric Science and the Science of Earth Systems.

She has also worked as a science educator, presenter, and exhibit planner at the Museum of Science in Boston, the Franklin Institute Science Museum, the Blue Hill Weather Observatory, and Yellowstone National Park.
