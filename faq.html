FAQs

Quick answers before you book.

The big idea: new families start with a free 30-minute virtual meeting, then book tutoring only after confirming it’s a good fit.

What happens if I’m interested in tutoring?

Book a free 30-minute virtual meeting with Lis. If the student is a minor, a parent or guardian should attend too. You’ll talk through goals, what tutoring might look like, and whether it feels like a good fit.

Where does tutoring take place?

Students can be tutored online or in person in Lis’s tutoring space.

What do you charge?

Virtual tutoring is $90 per 55-minute session. In-person tutoring is $100 per 55-minute session. Lis also holds a few low-cost or no-cost spots for students whose families are struggling financially.

How long are tutoring sessions?

Tutoring sessions are 55 minutes long. The complimentary intro meeting is 30 minutes long.

What grades do you tutor?

Lis tutors students in sixth grade and up, including high school and college students.

What subjects do you tutor?

Lis tutors organizational skills, study skills, physics, AP Earth Science, math from 8th grade through college intro, English and writing support, and Hebrew for bar and bat mitzvah preparation.

Do new families book paid tutoring right away?

No. New families should complete the complimentary 30-minute meeting first. Current families can then book either virtual or in-person tutoring.

How do families pay?

Families pay when they schedule a tutoring session.

Ready to meet Lis?

The free consult is the best place to ask questions and decide whether tutoring makes sense.
